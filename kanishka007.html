Career

Bose worked as a professor of physics at the University of Dhaka from 1921 to 1945. He later returned to India and served at the University of Calcutta.

He is best known for his work on quantum mechanics in the early 1920s, in developing the foundation for Bose–Einstein statistics and the theory of the Bose–Einstein condensate. A Fellow of the Royal Society, he was awarded India's second highest civilian award, the Padma Vibhushan, in 1954 by the Government of India.

Contributions of satyendranath bose

Bose is best known for his work on quantum mechanics, particularly for his development of Bose-Einstein statistics. He collaborated with Albert Einstein in 1924 to extend the idea of statistical mechanics to particles with integer spin, now known as bosons.
